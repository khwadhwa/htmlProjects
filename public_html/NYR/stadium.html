Madison Square Garden


The World's Most Famous Arena




Madison Square Garden, otherwise known as "The World's Most Famous Arena," is located in Midtown Manhattan. Constructed in 1968, the Garden is the oldest sporting facility still in use to date. It also plays host to the New York Knicks, New York Liberty, and St. John's University Red Storm.

How to get here

By Train By Bus By Car
Via Long Island Rail Road:
Take any Long Island Rail Road branch to Penn Station and follow signs for Madison Square Garden
Via Subway:
Take the 1/2/3 or A/C/E trains to 34th Street-Penn Station 		Take the M34-SBS, Q32, M34, or M8 to any of the Garden's entry points, located on 7th Avenue between 34th and 31st Streets From New Jersey:
Take the Lincoln Tunnel to Manhattan and follow exits for 34th Street to 7th Avenue.
From the Bronx/Northern New York:
Use 12th Avenue to move down Manhattan towards 34th Street. Follow signs for 7th Avenue and Madison Square Garden/Penn Station.
From Southern New York Use West Street/12th Avenue to move up Manhattan towards 34th Street. Follow signs for 7th Avenue and Madison Square Garden/Penn Station.
From Long Island and Points East
Use the Queens-Midtown Tunnel to enter Manhattan and follow 33rd Street west towards 7th Avenue.
